Case 1

Knight Capital Trading Disaster (2012)

  • Error: When deploying new code, a legacy trading module wasn't correctly removed.
  • Failure: The old code was accidentally activated, executing massive unintended trades.
  • Hazard: System capable of auto-executing large trades without human supervision.
  • Risk: High frequency market means Likelihood = Almost Certain, Impact = Critical → Extreme Risk.
Lessons Learned:
1. Legacy code must be deleted completely, not just ignored.
2. Rigorous pre-deployment testing is not optional.
3. Automated systems need risk control mechanisms (like circuit breakers).
