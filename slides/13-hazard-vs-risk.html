Hazard vs Risk

A Hazard is a dangerous condition that exists. Risk asks what could happen if people are exposed to that hazard, and how serious the consequences would be.

Hazard = Dangerous State

Scenario: A cliff has no guardrails.

  • What exists? A condition that can cause harm
  • Nature: Static — it is simply there
  • System example: Self-driving system cannot detect pedestrians in rain

Risk = Probability × Impact

Scenario: 1000 people walk near that cliff every day in bad weather.

  • What matters? Likelihood of falling + severity of harm
  • Nature: Dynamic — it depends on exposure and consequences
  • System example: Deploying the system in a rainy city increases the chance of injury
