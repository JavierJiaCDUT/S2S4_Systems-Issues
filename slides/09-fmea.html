Methodology

FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)

Core idea: before problems happen, identify what could fail, what the effect would be, what causes it, and which issues deserve the highest priority.

Failure Mode

What could go wrong?

  • Payment service crashes
  • Sensor stops responding

Effect

What happens if it fails?

  • User cannot complete payment
  • Service interruption

Cause

Why might it happen?

  • High traffic overload
  • Poor error handling

S / O / D

Score severity, occurrence, and detection from 1–10.

Priority

Use the result to decide what must be fixed first.

Severity (S)

How serious are the consequences if the failure happens?

Occurrence (O)

How likely is the failure mode to happen?

Detection (D)

How likely are we to detect it before it reaches the user?

RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

The higher the RPN, the higher the priority to reduce or control that risk.
